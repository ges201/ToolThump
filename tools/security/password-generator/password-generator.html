Creating strong, unique passwords is crucial for protecting your online accounts from unauthorized access. Here are some key principles:

  • üìè
    Length is Key: Aim for at least 12-16 characters. The longer your password, the exponentially harder it is for attackers to crack.
  • üîÄ
    Mix It Up: A strong password includes a mix of character types: uppercase letters (A-Z), lowercase letters (a-z), numbers (0-9), and symbols (!@#$%).
  • üö´
    Avoid the Obvious: Never use personal information (birthdays, names), common words ("password123"), or keyboard patterns ("qwerty"). These are the first things attackers try.
  • ‚ú®
    Uniqueness Matters: Use a different, strong password for every important account. If one site is breached, this prevents attackers from accessing your other accounts.
  • üîê
    Use a Password Manager: Remembering dozens of complex passwords is hard. A reputable password manager (like the free and open-source Bitwarden) can securely store them for you.
  • üì±
    Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always turn on 2FA (or MFA) when available. It adds a critical layer of security by requiring a second verification code, usually from your phone.
